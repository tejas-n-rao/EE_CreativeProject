\section{Scope and Unit Conventions}
This methodology documents the implemented logic in the repository for estimating monthly household greenhouse gas emissions and carbon index scores. The core reporting unit is kgCO\(_2\)e per month.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Monthly emissions are computed at activity-line level and then aggregated.
  \item Annual emissions are computed only for index comparison.
  \item Per-capita benchmark references are in tCO\(_2\)e/person/year.
\end{itemize}

\section{Implemented Calculation Pipeline}
\subsection{Step 1: Input Capture and Normalization}
Two input modes are implemented:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Monthly form}: user enters monthly activity values directly.
  \item \textbf{Weekly accessible form}: values are converted to monthly equivalents before API submission.
\end{itemize}

Weekly to monthly conversion uses:
$$
\text{Monthly value} = \text{Weekly value} \times 4.345
$$

Flight conversion uses:
$$
\text{Monthly flight passenger-km} = \frac{\text{Flights per year} \times \text{Average flight distance (km)}}{12}
$$

Accessible-form household proxies:
$$
\text{Electricity}_{\text{kWh/month}} = 45 + 18 \cdot \text{household size} + 1.2 \cdot 30 \cdot \text{AC hours/day} + 25 \cdot \mathbf{1}_{\text{refrigerator}}
$$

$$
\text{Weekly water (L)} = 40 \cdot 7 \cdot \text{household size} + 50 \cdot \text{showers/week} + 70 \cdot \text{laundry loads/week}
$$

$$
\text{Water}_{\text{m}^3/\text{month}} = \frac{\text{Weekly water (L)} \times 4.345}{1000}
$$

$$
\text{LPG}_{\text{kg/month}} = \text{home-cooked meals/week} \times 0.08 \times 4.345
$$

Diet is converted to monthly person-days as:
$$
\text{Diet activity} = 30\ \text{person\_day/month}
$$

\subsection{Step 2: Emission Factor Resolution}
For each activity line \((\text{category}, \text{unit}, \text{region}, \text{date})\), the API resolves factors in this order:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Excel custom override} from \texttt{data/emission\_factors.xlsx}, when custom factor exists.
  \item \textbf{Database factor} (\texttt{emission\_factors} table) with region match and date validity.
  \item \textbf{WORLD fallback} if region-specific factor is unavailable.
  \item \textbf{Excel default row} as final fallback.
\end{enumerate}

At repository state inspected on March 26, 2026, the Excel sheet contains \textbf{no active custom override rows}; effective factors therefore align with seeded defaults.

\subsection{Step 3: Line Emissions and Aggregation}
For each activity \(i\):
$$
E_i = A_i \times EF_i
$$
where \(A_i\) is activity data in native unit and \(EF_i\) is kgCO\(_2\)e per activity unit.

Total monthly footprint:
$$
E_{\text{monthly}} = \sum_i E_i
$$

Implementation detail:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Each line \(E_i\) is rounded to 4 decimal places.
  \item Total is the sum of rounded lines, then rounded to 4 decimal places.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Step 4: Annualization and Carbon Index}
Annualization:
$$
E_{\text{annual, kg}} = 12 \cdot E_{\text{monthly}}
$$
$$
E_{\text{annual, t}} = \frac{E_{\text{annual, kg}}}{1000}
$$

Index formulas:
$$
\text{India Index} = \frac{E_{\text{annual, t}}}{B_{\text{India}}} \times 100
$$
$$
\text{World Index} = \frac{E_{\text{annual, t}}}{B_{\text{World}}} \times 100
$$

Indices are rounded to 2 decimal places.

\subsection{Step 5: Class-Level Benchmark Comparison (Dashboard)}
Categories are grouped for class comparison:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Transportation}: petrol, diesel, two-wheeler, bus, metro, rail, short-haul flight.
  \item \textbf{Food}: diet categories + LPG.
  \item \textbf{Water}: water supply.
\end{itemize}

Class user annual tonnes:
$$
E_{\text{class, annual, t}} = \frac{12 \cdot E_{\text{class, monthly, kg}}}{1000}
$$

\section{Custom Factor Derivation Methods}
\subsection{Diet Factors (Custom)}
The repository's custom estimation note (\texttt{data/Custom\_Estimation.md}) derives diet factors from food-level life cycle assessment (LCA) intensities, primarily Poore and Nemecek (2018).

General diet construction formula:
$$
EF_{\text{diet}} = \sum_j \left(\text{intake}_{j,\text{kg/day}} \times EF_{j,\text{kgCO}_2\text{e/kg}}\right)
$$

Raw archetype outputs documented in the repository:
$$
EF_{\text{meat-heavy, raw}} = 11.9\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/day}
$$
$$
EF_{\text{mixed, raw}} = 5.85\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/day}
$$
$$
EF_{\text{plant-based, raw}} = 2.85\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/day}
$$

After adjustment for practical consumption effects (food waste, edible portion correction, retail-versus-consumption mass, and caloric normalization), the calculator uses:
$$
EF_{\text{diet, meat-heavy}} = 4.85\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/person-day}
$$
$$
EF_{\text{diet, mixed}} = 2.75\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/person-day}
$$
$$
EF_{\text{diet, plant-based}} = 1.80\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/person-day}
$$

\subsection{LPG Factor (Custom)}
The LPG factor is derived via NCV and pollutant factors (CO\(_2\), CH\(_4\), N\(_2\)O), then converted to CO\(_2\)e with GWP multipliers.

General conversion:
$$
EF_{\text{fuel, kgCO}_2\text{e/kg}} =
\text{NCV}_{\text{TJ/kg}}
\left[
EF_{\text{CO}_2,\text{t/TJ}}
 + EF_{\text{CH}_4,\text{t/TJ}} \cdot GWP_{\text{CH}_4}
 + EF_{\text{N}_2\text{O},\text{t/TJ}} \cdot GWP_{\text{N}_2\text{O}}
\right]
$$

Repository calculation constants:
\begin{itemize}
  \item NCV \(= 47.3\ \text{TJ/kt} = 0.0473\ \text{TJ/kg}\)
  \item \(EF_{\text{CO}_2}=63.1\ \text{t/TJ}\), \(EF_{\text{CH}_4}=0.005\ \text{t/TJ}\), \(EF_{\text{N}_2\text{O}}=0.0001\ \text{t/TJ}\)
  \item \(GWP_{\text{CH}_4}=28\), \(GWP_{\text{N}_2\text{O}}=265\)
\end{itemize}

Computed components:
$$
\text{CO}_2 = 63.1 \times 0.0473 = 2.9836\ \text{kgCO}_2/\text{kg}
$$
$$
\text{CH}_4\text{ as CO}_2\text{e} = (0.005 \times 0.0473)\times 28 = 0.00662\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/kg}
$$
$$
\text{N}_2\text{O as CO}_2\text{e} = (0.0001 \times 0.0473)\times 265 = 0.00125\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/kg}
$$
$$
EF_{\text{LPG}} = 2.9836 + 0.00662 + 0.00125 \approx 2.99\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/kg}
$$

\subsection{Metro Factor (Custom Note)}
The custom note indicates a planning range of approximately \(0.03\) to \(0.05\) kgCO\(_2\)e/passenger-km for electric urban rail/metro contexts, with midpoint selection rationale. The seeded operational value in this repository is:
$$
EF_{\text{metro}} = 0.041\ \text{kgCO}_2\text{e/km}
$$

\section{Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The model applies direct activity-factor multiplication and omits many second-order lifecycle effects.
  \item Several factors are scenario-based custom estimates (not universal regulatory constants), especially diet and LPG.
  \item Regional specificity is partial; unresolved regional gaps are intentionally filled by WORLD fallback factors.
  \item Benchmark years differ by metric (e.g., annual, transport, food, water), which may affect strict comparability.
  \item Accessible-form utility proxies are heuristic and may diverge from metered household data.
\end{itemize}
